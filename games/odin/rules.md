# Odin

## Players
From 2 to 6 players.

## Components
54 cards numbered 1 to 9 in 6 colours.


## OBJECT OF THE GAME

Be the first to discard all the cards from
your hand and have the fewest points at
the end of the game.

## SETUP

1. Shuffle all the cards.

2. Deal 9 cards face down to each player.
This is your hand. Feel free to look at your
hand, but don't let anyone else see! Set
any remaining cards aside. They will be
used later in the game.

Choose a first player at random. You wil
then take turns in clockwise order.

## 2 PLAYER VARIANT

For 2 players, we recommend removing
2 colours of cards to make the game quicker
and more intense!


## HOW TO PLAY
The game is played over several hands, each hand is divided into rounds. 
At the start of a round, if you are the first player, play 1 of your cards face up in the middle of the table.
Each player then takes turns to either: 
1. Play 1 or more card
Or
2. Pass


### 1. Play 1 or more cards
Play 1 or more cards following the restrictions below:
- You can play either the same number of cards as already in the middle of the table, or 1 card more.
- If you want to play multiple cards, they must be of the same number or the same colour.
    
    Example: if there is already a set of 2 cards, you can play a set of 2 cards or a set of 3 cards. However, you cannot play a set of 4 cards. You also cannot play 1 card on its own.

- The value of the cards you play must be greater than the value of those already in the middle of the table.

    Example: if the value in the middle of the table is 3, you must play a value of 4 or higher.

- When you play multiple cards, calculate their value by using each card number as a digit to create the highest number possible.

    Example: if you play 2 and 8 of the same colour, the value of the set is 82 (not 28). If you play 2, 4, and 9 - also of the same colour - the value of the set is 942.

Once you have played 1 or more cards, you must pick up 1 card from the middle of the table. You cannot pick up a card you have just played. Therefore:
	- lf there is only 1 card, you must pick it up.
	- If there is a set of cards, choose 1 card and discard the rest of the set.
	

### 2. Pass
Do not play any cards, and pass your turn to the next player.
Note: Even if you pass, it only applies to this turn, so you can play again on your next turn.
If all but 1 player pass consecutively, discard the remaining cards in the middle of the table. Keep the cards you have in your hand. The last person to place cards starts the new round.

### END OF A HAND
A hand can end in 2 ways:
- If you start a new round and all the cards in your hand are of the same number or of the same colour, you may play them all at once. The hand ends immediately after. If you cannot do this, just play 1 card. 
- If, at any point in the game, you play 1 or more cards and your hand is now empty, do not take a card from the middle of the table. The hand ends immediately.

At the end of a hand, you receive point for each card left in your hand. Keep note of your points.

To start a new hand, collect all the cards and shuffle them. Deal 9 cards to each player. The new hand begins with the player to the left of the player who started the previous hand. The new starting player plays 1 card.

### END OF THE GAME

If you are playing the game for the first
time, we recommend playing to 15 points,
Therefore, once player has 15 points or
more, the game ends. The player with the
fewest points wins! In case of a tie, those
players share the victory.

After your first game, you can choose the
number of points you want to play to. For
long games, you could play to 20. For short
games, to 10. For super short games, just
play 1 hand!
